Selected Projects
01
Jan 2026 — Feb 2026
Monolithic 3D JSCAN Architecture with RL Optimization

Designed a tier-aware 3D Joint-Scan architecture for monolithic multi-tier ICs integrating MSS and PRAS scan structures with a Global Test Controller (GTC). Developed a PPO-based multi-agent RL framework to optimise flip-flop clustering during physical design. Achieved 38% routing congestion reduction, 69% wirelength reduction, 87% peak test power reduction, and 20% area improvement on S-ISCAS89 benchmarks via complete RTL-to-GDS ASIC flow in Cadence Genus & Innovus.

May 2025 — Nov 2025
Tri-Directional Fabric (TDF) using 12T SRAM

Proposed a novel 12T SRAM-based in-memory computing architecture enabling native row, column, and diagonal data access to eliminate transposition bottlenecks in 2D workloads. Mapped image processing kernels (morphological dilation, Sobel operator, unsharp masking) achieving 1.77× speedup over CPU and 56.38% energy savings in 45nm CMOS post-layout simulations.

01

Correctness before cleverness. Every design must be verifiable. A fast chip that misbehaves is just a fast bug. Simulation, formal methods, and testbench discipline are not afterthoughts — they are the work.

02

ML and hardware are converging. Reinforcement learning for physical design, neural branch predictors, AI-assisted EDA — the next decade of chip design will be driven by learning-based optimisation. I build exactly at that intersection.

03

Publish to advance the field. Good research doesn't end at the waveform. Every architectural idea deserves scrutiny, and sharing accelerates progress for everyone working on the same hard problems.
